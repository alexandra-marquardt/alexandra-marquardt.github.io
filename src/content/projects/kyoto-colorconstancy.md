At the Kamitani Lab for AI in Neuroscience at Kyoto University, I investigated color constancy by reconstructing perceived visual stimuli from fMRI brain activity. The project uses deep learning-based reconstruction pipelines to test whether decoded images reflect the physical input or the color that a participant actually perceives under illusion-inducing conditions.

## Highlights

- Collected fMRI data while participants viewed stimuli designed to induce color constancy effects and color illusions.
- Reconstructed images from brain activity using deep learning pipelines and observed outputs aligned with perceived rather than physical color.
- Established a proof of concept that reconstruction models can capture individual perceptual experience instead of merely retrieving memorized image statistics.

> **Note:** This research is ongoing. Results on the underlying brain mechanisms of color constancy will be shared after completion.
