During my research internship at Brainlab AG, I developed 3D modeling and augmented reality software to help physicians adapt vascular prostheses to patient-specific anatomy. The project supported real-world clinical workflows and resulted in the peer-reviewed publication "Feasibility of Mixed Reality-assisted Physician-modified Endografts" in the Journal of Vascular Surgery: Cases, Innovations and Techniques.


## Highlights
- Built software to generate 3D models of patient-specific vascular prostheses from radiological measurements.
- Developed a Magic Leap 2 workflow that aligns virtual patient-specific models with baseline prostheses during modification.
- Worked with clinicians from Heidelberg University Hospital during clinical validation in realistic settings.
- Co-authored the resulting peer-reviewed publication in the Journal of Vascular Surgery: Cases, Innovations and Techniques.
