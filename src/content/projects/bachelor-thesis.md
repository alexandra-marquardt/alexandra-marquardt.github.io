In my bachelor's thesis at the lab of Prof. Nassir Navab (TUM), I analyzed how different ultrasound segmentation architectures distribute attention and how those internal patterns relate to model performance. The project combined comparative experiments across multiple datasets with Grad-CAM-based interpretation to better understand why some models generalize more effectively than others.

## Highlights

- Compared attention mechanisms of several state-of-the-art segmentation architectures across multiple ultrasound datasets.
- Used Grad-CAM to identify and interpret characteristic attention patterns in each model family.
- Connected observed attention behavior to differences in segmentation performance and model robustness.
