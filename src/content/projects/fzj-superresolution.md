At the Institute for Structural and Functional Organization of the Brain at Forschungszentrum Jülich, I developed a generative super-resolution pipeline for polarized light imaging data. Using paired macroscopic and microscopic scans, the model learned to reconstruct plausible fine-grained tissue structure from coarse input images at a 32x resolution increase.

## Highlights

- Built a paired dataset of macroscopic and microscopic brain scans acquired with polarized light imaging.
- Trained and evaluated a generative adversarial network for super-resolution from macro-scale to micro-scale tissue imagery.
- Reconstructed plausible microscopic structures that are not directly visible in the macroscopic scans.
